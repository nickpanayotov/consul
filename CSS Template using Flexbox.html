Part I - Introduction

1.Historical evolution

Consular posts have a longer and more varied history than diplomatic missions. Over time they have performed a variety of tasks—diplomatic, political, commercial, judicial, and maritime—all of which have left their mark on the activity of the modern consul. The consular institution developed out of the necessities of international trade. By generating a sense of confidence conducive to trade, travel, and residence in foreign lands, the consul grew in importance until today we find consuls of many lands in major cities of the world.
